Encryptids

Encryptids is an awareness campaign centered around creative AI and the current scam culture centered around it. The project gives creatives an approachable way to look into AI themselves and determine how they want to engage with it in the future. The project serves as a bridge between an awareness of current problems facing creatives with AI and education on AI technologies they may be unaware of.

The project features a team of cryptids that each represent a nefarious part of scam culture taking place within creative AI technologies. The same way cryptids were used in folklore to warn people and bring awareness to certain behaviors, Encryptids will strive to do the same in our modern world of creative AI. Through mythical storytelling based in some reality, encrypitds will show people the current nefarious activities happening online in various creative fields while also encouraging them to look into these areas further themselves.

In order to grab the attention of other creatives the branding of encryptids needs to be engaging as well as approachable when tackling heavy topics such as AI art.

Research

Process & User Feedback

  • Sketches

    This project would be heavily anchored on character illustrations and animation. Due to this, a large portion of the project was dedicated to character sketches and storyboard sketches to pave the way for the encryptids story. .

  • Sketches

    As the design concepts began to crystalize into something more substantial, I moved from analogue drawing techniques to digital in order to experiment with color and motion effects. The extensive sketching phase created a deeper understanding of each character and ultimately what each one would represent.

  • WireFrames

    Along with sketching and animating characters, I knew the final landing spot for the characters and story would be on a website that both explained my process as well as told the story of the encryptids. Because of this, I wanted to have a solid wireframe of the website and each of its storytelling components.

  • User Feedback

    During the process of creating the encryptids I reached out to other creatives and creative agencies to gauge how they were feeling and reacting to AI becoming more common in the workplace. All of the responses centered around apprehensive and daunted by how quickly AI was taking over tasks and job positions.

  • External Feedback

    As well as target audience feedback, I reached out to experts in the field that had a better understanding of AI not just in a creative lens but also a technical and security sense. I was able to get a helpful insight into more uncommon problems that AI creates for people who engage with it online and what information is collected when using it. These insights helped in creating the stories and character backgrounds the encrypted character’s inherit.

Next Steps
